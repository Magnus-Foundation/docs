## Why the category exists

Stablecoin volume exceeded ACH for the first time in February 2026, reaching \$7.2T/month. The global fiat-backed stablecoin supply crossed \$273B in March 2026, a 40x growth in 6 years. Real-world stablecoin payment volume doubled in 2025 to \$400B, with 60% estimated to be B2B.

With regulatory clarity arriving (MiCA in the EU, GENIUS Act in the US, Vietnam's Resolution 05/2025), capital is consolidating around infrastructure that can handle this volume at scale. That's why \$1.1B landed in stablecoin chains in under 12 months.

## What each chain is optimized for

**Tempo:** The enterprise payment chain. Partners include Visa, Deutsche Bank, Standard Chartered, OpenAI, Anthropic, Shopify, DoorDash. MPP (Machine Payments Protocol) for AI agent streaming payments. Stripe's distribution is the moat. USD-centric, US/Western market.

**Arc:** The institutional USDC chain. BlackRock's BUIDL fund is the implied flagship. Apollo's private credit distribution. 100+ institutional partners. The chain for tokenized real-world assets denominated in USD. Not for USDT users, not for emerging markets.

**Plasma:** The USDT volume chain. Launched with \$2B in USDT0 liquidity day one via LayerZero OFT. Peaked at \$5.6B TVL in week one. Currently \$599M TVL (April 2026). Most of the incentive-driven liquidity has left. Real adoption is harder than subsidized deposits.

**Codex:** The neutral Ethereum L2. Backed by Dragonfly, Coinbase Ventures, and Circle Ventures. Multi-issuer USDC and USDT support. Trades Magnus's L1 performance for Ethereum security guarantees.

## Where Magnus sits

Magnus is not competing with Tempo for enterprise payments or with Arc for institutional USDC flows. Those markets are well-served and well-funded.

Magnus targets what they all ignore:

**Geographic gap.** Tempo serves US/Western institutional. Arc serves BlackRock-aligned TradFi. Plasma is general-purpose USDT volume. None of them are building rails for Vietnam, Indonesia, the Philippines, Brazil, or Kenya, where USDT adoption is growing fastest and local fiat integration is the unsolved problem.

**Technical gap.** Every competing chain requires users to manage a separate gas token (or relies on paymasters that add complexity). Magnus's protocol-native multi-currency fee layer means users pay gas in whatever stablecoin they hold. This is not possible on existing chains, as it requires controlling the L1's fee mechanism.

**Issuer gap.** Arc is USDC-controlled. Plasma is USDT-controlled. Tempo is Stripe-controlled. No competing chain can claim neutrality without contradicting their own investors. Magnus has no issuer at the cap table.

## The AI agent opportunity

As of May 2026, the AI agent payment infrastructure race is also live:
- **x402** (Coinbase, open source): 140M agent transactions, \$43M volume, 400K+ agents, 98.6% USDC on Base
- **MPP** (Stripe + Tempo): streaming payments for agents, launched March 2026
- **AWS Bedrock AgentCore Payments**: launched May 7, 2026 with Coinbase + Stripe

All of these are USDC-on-Base or USD-centric. None support multi-currency agent payments for users outside the US/Western context. Magnus's x402 implementation supports agents paying both service fees and gas in the same non-USD stablecoin: mVND, mEUR, or any registered MIP-20.

## Route 66: the integration standard

On May 12, 2026, Commonware and Coinbase announced Route 66, a joint initiative with a Coinbase Ventures strategic investment to standardize exchange, wallet, and custodian integration for specialized blockchains like Magnus. Magnus is a Route 66 participant chain.

This directly addresses the main barrier to exchange adoption: Magnus's custom transaction type (type-0x76) and precompiles require specialized integration work. Route 66 provides the standard libraries and tooling to reduce this friction across the Coinbase ecosystem.
