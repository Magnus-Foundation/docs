Magnus is the neutral digital currency infrastructure for emerging markets.

The past 12 months have seen Circle, Tether, and Stripe each launch their own stablecoin chain — Arc (USDC-aligned, $3B, backed by BlackRock and Apollo), Plasma (USDT-aligned, $373M, backed by Tether and Founders Fund), and Tempo (Stripe-Bridge-aligned, $5B). Each one is structurally aligned to a single issuer. On Circle's chain, USDT is a second-class citizen. On Tether's chain, USDC is unwelcome.

Magnus is the counter-position: the chain where every stablecoin is first-class, and where you pay gas in whatever you already hold.

## Three things Magnus does that others don't

**Gas in your currency.** Send USDT, pay gas in mVND. No ETH. No TRX. No separate gas token to buy. This is built into the protocol — not a paymaster workaround, not per-app account abstraction. Every wallet, every transaction, automatic.

**Issuer-neutral by design.** No single stablecoin issuer sits at the cap table. USDC, USDT, EURC, USDe, PYUSD, and regional stables like mVND — all registered and treated equally by the protocol.

**Built for emerging markets.** The $19B/year Vietnam remittance corridor. The 5 newly licensed Vietnamese exchanges under the 2026 crypto sandbox. VietQR, UPI, PIX, and M-Pesa as protocol-native operations — not third-party integrations. The markets where USDT dominates, USDC is growing, and neither Arc nor Tempo is building.

## Built on Commonware

Magnus is built on [Commonware](https://commonware.xyz), a modular blockchain infrastructure toolkit. On May 12, 2026, Commonware announced Route 66 — a joint initiative with Coinbase (strategic investment from Coinbase Ventures) to standardize exchange and wallet integration for specialized blockchains.

Magnus is a Route 66 participant chain. Exchange and wallet integration uses Route 66 standards, backed by the Coinbase ecosystem.

Commonware provides:
- **Simplex BFT** — 300ms deterministic finality, no reorgs
- **BLS12-381 threshold signatures** — for the MBS cross-chain bridge
- **Production-grade P2P** — battle-tested networking

## The long game

Magnus's near-term wedge is AI agent payments and the Vietnam remittance corridor. The long-term target is digital currency infrastructure for Southeast Asian central banks — the same compliance precompile, BFT consensus, and multi-currency support that payment apps use today is what a CBDC pilot requires tomorrow.
